Open Neuroimaging Laboratory


Loads of imaging data are available online
thanks to goodwill of thousands of participants and funding bodies,
but the data are hard to find, vary in their quality,
and are difficult to aggregate for analyses.

This is why we are creating the Open Neuroimaging Lab!
An online platform for collaborative, crowdsourced analysis.
The Lab will help train scientists and engage the public using
game-like apps, and improve transparency, statistical power,
and reproducibility of science.

The Lab will provide Collective analysis tools
for researchers. An easy and fast access encourages sharing of data,
and the ability to create and curate catalogues of shared data in
real-time and social interaction. A first citizen science project
within the Lab is the app BrainBox.

The browser-based application allows individuals to work
together remotely, on a wide array of devices and diverse data from animal species,
clinical populations, and unique individuals, without having to download complete
datasets or install software. The app will index available data as they are used,
and store annotation and analysis results for reuse.
